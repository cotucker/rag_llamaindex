The planet Mars has always attracted humanity's attention with its reddish hue, visible to the naked eye. Mars' atmosphere is very thin and consists mainly of carbon dioxide, making it unbreathable for humans without special equipment. Mars rovers such as Curiosity and Perseverance have been exploring the planet's surface for years in search of traces of ancient water and microbial life. One of the main problems with colonizing Mars is the high level of radiation and low gravity, which is only 38% of Earth's. Scientists estimate that terraforming Mars could take hundreds or even thousands of years before the first plants can grow there.
